# Wyatt User Guide

## ðŸ‘¥ Customers & CRM

The Customers module allows you to manage the individuals and companies you do business with.

1.  **View Customers**: Navigate to **Customers** in the sidebar to see a list of all your clients. You can search the list and view key details like their primary contact email and phone number.
2.  **Add a Customer**: Click **New Customer** to add a record. You will need their name, company, email, and optionally, a phone number and address.
3.  **Customer Details**: Clicking on a customer will show their full history, including associated projects, estimates, and invoices.

## ðŸ—ï¸ Projects & Tasks

Projects are the core of Wyatt's tracking system. They allow you to organize work, track budgets, and associate time entries and invoices.

1.  **Project List**: View all active, pending, and completed projects under the **Projects** tab.
2.  **Create a Project**: Click **New Project**. You'll assign it to a Customer, give it a name, set a budget, and choose a status.
3.  **Project Details**: Inside a project, you have access to several tabs:
    - **Details**: General information and status.
    - **Time Entries**: All hours logged against this specific project.
    - **Estimates**: Proposed costs associated with the project.
    - **Contacts**: Key stakeholders for this project.

## ðŸ’° Estimates & Quotes

Before beginning work, use the Estimates module to propose costs to your customers.

1.  **Create an Estimate**: Go to **Estimates -> New Estimate**.
2.  **Details**: Link the estimate to a specific project. Add line items detailing the service or product, quantity, and unit price. The total will calculate automatically.
3.  **Status**: Estimates start as 'Draft' and can be moved to 'Sent', 'Approved', or 'Rejected'.

### Logging Time
1.  Navigate to **Time Entries**.
2.  Click **Log Time**.
3.  Select the **Project** and **Task**.
4.  Enter the date and duration (in hours).
5.  Add a description of the work performed.

### Expense Reports
1.  Navigate to **Expense Reports**.
2.  Submit expenses incurred during your projects for tracking and potential reimbursement. You can tie these to specific projects and categorize them (e.g., Travel, Materials).

## ðŸ§¾ Billing & Invoices

Convert your hard work into revenue.

1.  **Generate an Invoice**: Go to **Invoices**. You can often generate these directly from approved Estimates or accumulated Time Entries within a Project.
2.  **Invoice Details**: Similar to Estimates, you'll add line items, terms, and notes. Choose between different templates (Standard, Modern, Simple).
3.  **Sending**: Invoices can be sent directly to the customer's email from within Wyatt.
4.  **Tracking**: Monitor invoice statuses (Draft, Sent, Paid, Overdue).
